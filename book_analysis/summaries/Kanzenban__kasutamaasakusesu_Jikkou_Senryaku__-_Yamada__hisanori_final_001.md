## 1. Introduction & Core Concepts

### *Customer Success* in the Subscription Era
The book is based on global CS theories refined through practical application (specifically at Sansan Corporation) and serves as a practical textbook rather than a conceptual启蒙 book. It targets **B2B SaaS** practices but applies to B2C as well. In the modern "Everything-as-a-Service" era, CS acts as the driver for business growth.

### The Shift from "Flow" to "Stock" Business Models
*   **Traditional SI (System Integration) Model:** A "flow" type business relying on one-off transactions.
*   **SaaS + Subscription Model:** A "stock" type business where revenue accumulates as long as the customer continues using the service.
    *   **Provider Benefits:** High revenue predictability.
    *   **User Benefits:** Automatic service evolution, period-based payment without large upfront investments.

### Customer Satisfaction vs. Customer Success
*   **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT):** A passive approach based on the premise that a "satisfied customer will continue." However, data shows satisfied customers often churn.
*   **Customer Success:** An aggressive ("offensive") approach committing to the customer's **Outcome** (desired results), not just satisfaction. 

> **Definition of Success**: A continuous "state" where the customer realizes the value of the product/service and continuously achieves their desired outcomes. 

#### The Two Mandatory Conditions for CS:
1.  **Identifiable Outcomes:** The ability to define what constitutes a valuable outcome for the customer.
2.  **Measurability:** The ability to measure whether the customer is achieving this outcome.

### The 4 Core Roles of CS
1.  **Churn Reduction:** Preventing cancellations.
2.  **Negative Churn (Expansion):** Driving upsells and cross-sells.
3.  **Second Order Revenue:** Generating referrals and advocacy.
4.  **Product Feedback Loop:** Collecting VoC (Voice of Customer) to improve the product.

## 2. Strategy & Frameworks

### Gainsight's Maturity Model
CS organizations evolve through four maturity levels, correlated with Net Retention Rate (NRR):
1.  **REACTIVE:** Ad-hoc, passive support.
2.  **INSIGHTS & ACTIONS:** Frameworks and data are used to create action plans.
3.  **OUTCOMES:** Proactively guiding customers to defined success states.
4.  **TRANSFORM:** A completely established, proprietary methodology where CS drives the business (achieving ~125% growth).

### The CS Elements (Gainsight Framework)
A periodic-table-style framework organizing CS activities into categories:
*   **INSIGHTS & ACTIONS:** 
    *   *360° Customer View [Cv]*: Aggregating customer data.
    *   *Lifecycle Management [Lm]*: Segmenting by usage duration.
    *   *Outcomes Health [Co]*: Scoring customer health.
*   **OUTCOMES:**
    *   *Adoption Management [Am]*: Driving service utilization.
    *   *Renewal Management [Rm]*: Ensuring contract continuation.
    *   *Success Planning [Sp]*: Creating roadmaps for customer goals.
*   **TRANSFORM:**
    *   *Services Experience [Se]*: Proving ROI.
    *   *Company Success [Cs]*: Using customer success as marketing.

### Customer Segmentation Strategy
CS strategies must scale based on customer size:
*   **High-Touch:** Dedicated CSMs for large enterprise accounts.
*   **Low-Touch:** 1 CSM manages hundreds of mid-market accounts; periodic support.
*   **Tech-Touch:** Automated, technology-driven engagement for small businesses/SMBs.

## 3. The Customer Lifecycle

The lifecycle is divided into distinct phases, typically based on time elapsed (Adoption Period Base) or customer status.

### 1. Onboarding (Introduction)
The most critical phase, acting as an "education period."
*   **Design Requirements:** Defined **Duration**, clear **Success Criteria** (measured quantitatively), and specific **Support Content**.
*   **Success Formula:** Standardization is key. Avoid over-customization.
*   **Paid Onboarding:** Charging for onboarding increases customer commitment and seriousness.
*   **Acceptance Structure:** Require the customer to designate a **Decision Maker**, **Promoter**, and **Operator** to prevent implementation failure.

### 2. Adoption (Utilization)
Bridging the gap between basic usage and deep business integration.
*   **Content Strategy:** Create use-case-specific content (reference manuals, success stories, journey formats) to drive deeper usage.
*   **Light Success vs. Deep Success:**
    *   *Light Success:* Frontline operational improvements (e.g., convenience).
    *   *Deep Success:* Management-level impact (e.g., cost reduction, revenue growth).
    *   *Rule:* Always achieve **Light Success** first as a stepping stone to Deep Success.

### 3. Renewal (Update)
Confirming continuation and preventing churn.
*   **The 8 Renewal Activities:** 
    1. Grasping the decision-making line, 2. Confirming renewal intent, 3. Rebuilding internal support, 4. Collecting VoC, 5. Reviewing usage, 6. Confirming ROI, 7. Checking goal progress, 8. Exploring expansion.

### 4. Churn (Cancellation)
Managing the exit gracefully to leave the door open for future return. Analyzing churn reasons is vital data.

## 4. Metrics & Health Scores

### CS Metrics (North Star Metric)
Traditional ROI is difficult to measure as it relies on customer subjectivity. **CS Metrics** serve as a leading indicator.
*   **Definition:** An indicator showing whether existing customers are using the service "smoothly and healthily."
*   **Design:** Expressed as a **ratio** (e.g., active users / total contracted users) rather than absolute numbers.
*   **Usage:** Functions as a KPI for the Adoption phase and a performance indicator for CSMs.

### Health Score Design
A numerical value indicating the risk of churn. Designed using the **DEAR Framework**:
*   **Deployment (D):** Are prerequisites set up?
*   **Engagement (E):** Relationship quality (qualitative).
*   **Adoption (A):** Product usage (e.g., WAU).
*   **ROI (R):** Value realization.

> **Important Note:** A Health Score is only valid if it aligns with the **qualitative intuition of the frontline CSM**. Distribution should be manageable (e.g., 15% Green, 45% Lime, 30% Yellow, 10% Red).

## 5. Technology & Automation

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)
A strategy where the product itself drives acquisition, retention, and expansion (e.g., Slack, Dropbox).
*   **4 Principles:** 1. Remove friction, 2. Deliver value quickly, 3. Remove unnecessary features, 4. Become indispensable.
*   **PQL (Product Qualified Lead):** A user who has experienced value (e.g., hit usage limits) and is highly likely to convert to paid.

### Tech-Touch Automation
Automating CS processes to scale efficiently.
*   **Ideal Targets:** Onboarding, Renewal prep, NPS surveys, Information delivery.
*   **Implementation:** Requires a combination of **automated delivery (emails)**, **self-content (media/videos)**, and **CS CTA (Call To Action)** alerts for manual intervention when automated steps fail.

## 6. Organizational Design & Talent

### Organizational Structure
CS departments typically consist of 5 units:
1.  **Customer Success Managers (CSM):** Frontline customer success drivers.
2.  **Customer Marketing:** Creating content and managing communities.
3.  **Operations (Ops):** Managing data infrastructure and workflows.
4.  **Technical Support:** Handling functional (reactive) inquiries.
5.  **Control Center:** Department-wide planning and talent development.

### Segment Design
*   **Lifecycle Specialization:** Dividing teams by phase (e.g., Onboarding team).
*   **Segment-Proximate:** Dividing by customer size (Enterprise vs. SMB).
*   *Best Practice:* Start with **Segment-Proximate** to build deep customer understanding, then shift to **Lifecycle Specialization** for efficiency.

### CSM Capacity Planning
*   **Benchmarks:** 
    *   *SMB:* ~50 accounts / CSM (ARR < 50M JPY).
    *   *Enterprise:* 10-50 accounts / CSM (ARR > 200M JPY).

### The 8 Traits of a CSM
*   **Personality:** Analytical thinking, Proactivity, Empathy, Persistence/Passion.
*   **Skills:** Team play, Communication, High professionalism, Strategic thinking.

## 7. Functional Integration (Ops, Marketing, Support)

### CS Operations (Ops)
Acts as the "CS for the CS team."
*   **Mission:** Creating weapons (content) and delivery systems (infrastructure) to boost CSM productivity.

### Customer Marketing
Focuses on existing customers (unlike traditional marketing).
*   **Role:** Standardizing CSM tasks by creating public channel content (newsletters, media) and private channel templates.
*   **Community Strategy:** Building user communities to generate case studies and advocacy.

### Technical Support (TS)
*   **Positioning Debate:** Should TS belong to the Product team (focus: self-help KBs) or the CS team (focus: Customer Experience)?
*   **Evolution:** Start TS in CS (Years 1-3), move to Product (Years 4-8) to build robust Knowledge Bases, then return to CS (Years 9+) to optimize CX.
*   **The Low Effort Trend:** Minimize "Customer Effort" rather than providing excessive "delight." 

### Product Marketing Manager (PMM)
A role that bridges Product Development and the Business side (Marketing/Sales/CS).
*   **Mission:** Accelerating **PMF (Product-Market fit)**.
*   **Tasks:** Defining go-to-market messages, setting KPIs for new features, preparing sales enablement content, and managing the product feedback loop.
