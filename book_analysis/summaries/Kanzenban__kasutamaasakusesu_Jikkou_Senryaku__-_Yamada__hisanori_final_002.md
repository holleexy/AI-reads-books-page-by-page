# カスタマーサクセス実行戦略 including Customer Success and Subscription Models

**Customer Success (CS)** is a proactive business approach focused on ensuring customers achieve their desired outcomes (*Outcomes*) while using a product or service. This concept has become the driving force behind modern business, particularly within the **SaaS (Software as a Service)** and **Subscription Model** paradigms.

- **The Shift to "Stock" Models:** Traditional "Flow" models (one-time transactions like System Integration) rely on constant new sales. In contrast, "Stock" models (SaaS + Subscription) generate stable, predictable recurring revenue based on continuous customer usage. 
- **Why CS Emerged:** In subscription models, profitability depends entirely on customer retention. If customers are merely *satisfied* but not achieving tangible results, they will eventually churn. CS evolved to bridge this gap by committing to the customer's success, not just their satisfaction.
- **Two Core Conditions for CS Implementation:**
  1. **Identifiable Outcomes:** The business must be able to identify the specific valuable outcomes the customer seeks.
  2. **Measurable Success:** It must be possible to measure whether the customer is actually achieving this success state.

> **Note:** The ultimate goal of CS is to deliver an *Outcome* to the customer. *Success* is defined as the continuous state where the customer realizes this outcome. Delivering value is the provider's responsibility, but the specific outcome is highly individualized based on the customer's business context.

## 2. Frameworks and Best Practices

### Gainsight's Customer Success Maturity Levels
CS organizations typically evolve through four maturity stages:
1. **REACTIVE:** Ad-hoc responses to customer inquiries.
2. **INSIGHTS & ACTIONS:** Frameworks are built based on data (e.g., 360° Customer View, Lifecycle Management).
3. **OUTCOMES:** Proactively driving customer success through Adoption Management, Renewal Management, and Expansion Management.
4. **TRANSFORM:** Fully controlling the customer lifecycle, turning CS into a major profit center with exceptional retention rates.

### Engagement Models (Customer Pyramid)
Customers should be segmented to allocate resources efficiently:
- **High-Touch:** Dedicated CSMs for large enterprise accounts.
- **Low-Touch:** CSMs managing hundreds of mid-market accounts with standardized support.
- **Tech-Touch:** Automated, technology-driven engagement for small businesses (SMBs).

### The Customer Lifecycle
The relationship is managed through distinct phases:
1. **Onboarding:** The most critical phase. The goal is to deliver the product's core value and ensure initial setup (Duration: typically 1-3 months).
2. **Adoption (Utilization):** The customer integrates the product into their daily workflows.
3. **Renewal:** The phase where contract continuation is confirmed and expansion opportunities are explored.
4. **Churn (Cancellation):** Managing the exit gracefully to leave the door open for future re-engagement and collecting data to understand churn reasons.

## 3. Core Concepts and Metrics

### Light Success vs. Deep Success
Success occurs in stages. Understanding this helps set proper expectations:
- **Light Success:** Immediate operational benefits at the frontline level (e.g., convenience, time-saving). Securing this ensures short-term retention.
- **Deep Success:** Long-term business transformation and ROI at the executive level. This secures multi-year retention.

### Health Scores
A metric (or collection of metrics) used to determine the "health" or renewal likelihood of an account.
- **DEAR Framework:** A design methodology measuring **D**eployment (setup completeness), **E**ngagement (relationship strength), **A**doption (usage rates), and **R**OI (value realization).
- **Design Process:** Should blend quantitative data (usage logs) with the qualitative intuition of frontline CSMs. Scores must be continuously validated against past churn data.

### CS Metrics and ROI
While **North Star Metrics** guide company-wide growth, **CS Metrics** focus purely on the healthy, frictionless usage of the service by existing customers.
- **CS Metric Formula:** Design metrics as ratios (e.g., `Active Features / Total Licensed Users`) rather than absolute numbers to account for customer growth.
- **Calculating ROI:** Customers often struggle to articulate ROI. Providers should build logical formulas connecting CS Metrics to increased sales or reduced costs and validate them with the customer.

### Churn (Cancellation) Management
Churn is categorized into *Controllable* (e.g., poor onboarding) and *Uncontrollable* (e.g., customer bankruptcy). 
- **Renewal Management (Churn Interception):** Executing 8 key activities prior to contract renewal, including: identifying decision-makers, confirming support structures, collecting VoC, and proving ROI.
- **Risk Escalation:** Setting up systems (e.g., CS CTA alerts) to monitor stakeholder changes (like champion departures) that signal uncontrollable churn risk.

## 4. Technology and Automation

### The CS Tech Stack
Implementing CS requires integrating various tools to break down data silos:
1. **CRM / Master Record:** (e.g., Salesforce) for customer data.
2. **CS Platforms:** (e.g., Gainsight, Totango) for health scoring and workflow.
3. **Product Usage Tools:** (e.g., Tableau) for data visualization.
4. **Communication / Marketing:** (e.g., Marketo, Slack, Zoom) for engagement.

### CS CTA (Call To Action)
Automated alerts triggered by specific data thresholds (e.g., "Usage dropped below 20%"). This mechanism allows CSMs to manage large portfolios efficiently without constantly monitoring dashboards.

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)
A strategy where the product itself drives acquisition, retention, and expansion.
- **Key Principles:** Frictionless onboarding (Free trial/Freemium), virality (in-product sharing), scalable pricing, and in-app upgrades.
- **PQL (Product Qualified Lead):** A user who has experienced value within the product and is ready for a sales conversation. PQLs convert significantly higher than traditional Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs).

## 5. Organizational Design and Operations

### Building the CS Team
A mature CS division typically comprises 5 functional units:
1. **Customer Success Managers (CSMs):** Frontline customer support.
2. **Customer Marketing:** Content creation, media management, and community building.
3. **Operations (CS Ops):** Data infrastructure, workflow design, and system maintenance.
4. **Technical Support:** Resolving functional issues (Level 1/2 support).
5. **Control Center:** Strategic planning, KPI management, and training.

### Unit Segmentation Strategies
- **Lifecycle-Specific:** Teams dedicated solely to Onboarding or Renewal (high efficiency).
- **Segment-Specific:** CSMs dedicated to specific tiers or industries (high relationship depth).
- **Volume Guidelines:** Benchmarks suggest ~50 accounts per CSM for mid-market, or <$5M ARR per CSM.

### Technical Support Evolution
- **Early Phase (1-3 yrs):** Support is part of CS (Focus: CX).
- **Growth Phase (4-8 yrs):** Support moves to Product (Focus: Building Knowledge Base/FAQs).
- **Mature Phase (9+ yrs):** Support returns to CS (Focus: LTV maximization).
- **Modern Trend:** Lowering "Customer Effort" is prioritized over simply "delighting" the customer.

## 6. Product Marketing Management (PMM)

As organizations scale, the responsibility for aligning the product with the market shifts from the CSM to the **Product Marketing Manager (PMM)**.

- **Role Clarity:** 
  - **PdM (Product Manager):** Responsible for *building* the right product.
  - **PMM (Product Marketing Manager):** Responsible for *fitting* the product to the market.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
  1. Defining marketing messages for new features.
  2. Setting and tracking KPIs for feature adoption.
  3. Equipping Sales and CS with necessary content.
  4. Establishing the Voice of Customer (VoC) feedback loop.
- **CS & PMM Synergy:** While PMM defines the strategy for Product-Market Fit (PMF) acceleration, CS remains the primary gatherer of raw customer insights (VoC) required to fuel that strategy.

At its core, it is about building fundamental human trust through business activities.
